Hawaii's Favorite Snack!

Description

In Hawaii, spam musubis are eaten for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. We eat them on the plane, at school or work, during sporting events, at the beach, and at home. You might not like spam but when you eat a spam musubi you will love spam. The spam musubi comes from the old plantation days and has evolved to become Hawaii's favorite snack!

Ingredients

  • Spam
  • Nori
  • Rice
  • Soy sauce
  • Brown sugar

Steps

  1. Cook the rice.
  2. While rice is cooking, cut the spam into 1/4 inch slices.
  3. When rice is finished cook the spam over the stove until slightly crispy and brown.
  4. While cooking the spam place soy sauce and brown sugar in a separate pan and heat over the stove until sauce thickens.
  5. Cut the sheets of nori in half.
  6. Place spam musubi maker on top of the half sheet of nori.
  7. Fill spam musubi maker with rice 3/4 to the top.
  8. Spread soy sauce mixture generously over the rice.
  9. Coat slice of spam with soy sauce mixture and place spam into the musubi maker on top of the rice.
  10. Take the other part of the spam musubi maker and compress the spam and rice dow.n
  11. Remove musubi maker and roll the nori to close.
  12. Give thanks and enjoy.
